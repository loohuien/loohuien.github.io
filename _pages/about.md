My name is Loo Hui En (pronounced as Hwee-Urn), an incoming PhD student at the iSchool at the [University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign](https://illinois.edu/) starting Fall 2026. My research interests spans across **human–machine communication, information integrity and responsible AI**. I am driven by a desire to understand how we can harness the power of technology to foster positive change for all.

I grew up in Singapore 🇸🇬 and received my early training in Chinese Media and Communication at [Ngee Ann Polytechnic](https://www.np.edu.sg/). I subsequently graduated from [Nanyang Technological University](https://www.ntu.edu.sg/) with a bachelor's degree in Public Policy and Global Affairs. Prior to enrolling at NTU, I worked as a journalist at [Lianhe Zaobao](https://www.zaobao.com.sg/) (2020–2021), covering local and crime news.

At NTU, I have been fortunate to work with several mentors who shaped my research journey. I first worked with [Dr Jeremy Sng](https://dr.ntu.edu.sg/entities/person/Jeremy-Sng) on my first independent research project on **generative AI and educational inequality**. I subsequently worked with [A/P Wang Jue](https://dr.ntu.edu.sg/entities/person/Wang-Jue), whose guidance deepened my interests in **students' behavioural responses to AI in education**, followed by [A/P Chou Meng-Hsuan](https://www.menghsuanchou.com/about) as a research assistant. I am currently a research assistant at the **SWEET Lab**, directed by [Asst Prof. Renwen Zhang](https://dr.ntu.edu.sg/entities/person/Renwen-Zhang), where I conduct research on **AI-mediated communication and AI and mental health**.
